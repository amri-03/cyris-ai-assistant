# Behavior Tracking Concepts

## Overview

Axis AI Assistant should gradually understand how the user behaves over time instead of relying only on manually entered tasks or schedules.

The goal is to identify patterns that affect:

* consistency
* focus
* direction
* productivity
* motivation stability

Behavior tracking is intended to support adaptive guidance rather than surveillance.

---

# Important Behavioral Signals

## Activity Consistency

Track:

* how regularly users engage with important focus areas
* periods of inactivity
* repeated disappearance from goals or routines

---

## Motivation Cycles

Identify:

* sudden motivation spikes
* rapid loss of momentum
* repeated project abandonment patterns

---

## Focus Shifts

Observe:

* changing interests
* neglected priorities
* recurring return to certain goals or domains

---

## Productivity Timing

Track:

* productive time windows
* preferred work periods
* low-focus periods
* schedule-related consistency changes

---

# Behavioral Goals

The behavioral system should help Axis:

* detect disengagement early
* reduce direction drift
* suggest realistic re-entry actions
* reduce overwhelm
* support consistency recovery

---

# Intervention Philosophy

Axis should avoid:

* excessive notifications
* aggressive productivity pressure
* guilt-driven reminders
* overwhelming task suggestions

Interventions should remain:

* lightweight
* contextual
* calm
* practical
* selective

---

# Examples

Examples of future behavior-aware interventions:

* suggesting small re-entry tasks after inactivity
* reducing workload suggestions during busy periods
* prioritizing realistic goals during exams or stress
* detecting repeatedly abandoned focus areas
