# Priority Engine Concepts

## Overview

The Priority Engine is responsible for helping Cyris determine what should matter most at a given moment.

The system should help users manage multiple ambitions realistically instead of forcing them into rigid productivity systems.

---

# Core Purpose

The priority system should:

* reduce cognitive overload
* adapt to changing situations
* rebalance focus areas dynamically
* help users avoid direction drift
* prioritize realistic action

---

# Priority Factors

Cyris may eventually consider:

* deadlines
* career opportunities
* financial needs
* internship preparation
* academic workload
* free time availability
* energy levels
* consistency history
* long-term importance
* urgency
* burnout risk

---

# Adaptive Prioritization

Priorities should change dynamically based on:

* current life situation
* temporary workload
* schedule changes
* behavioral consistency
* inactivity periods
* upcoming opportunities

Examples:

* interview preparation may temporarily outrank exploratory learning
* recovery tasks may be prioritized after burnout or disengagement
* smaller tasks may be prioritized during low-energy periods

---

# Goal Of The Priority Engine

The system should not maximize busyness.

Instead, it should:

* improve alignment
* improve clarity
* reduce mental overload
* support realistic execution
* preserve sustainable progress

---

# Important Constraints

The priority engine should avoid:

* overwhelming the user
* constant reprioritization
* unrealistic planning
* excessive intervention
* rigid scheduling behavior

Cyris should remain adaptive and supportive rather than controlling.
